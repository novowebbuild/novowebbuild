Does my business need Google Maps?

8 May 2026 4 min read Novo Web Build

Someone is walking down the street. They're hungry, or looking for a hotel, or need a plumber. They open their phone and type it into Google. What do they see? A map with three businesses. If your business is not one of those three, you do not exist for that person.

Google Maps is not just a navigation app. It is one of the most powerful free marketing tools available to any small business. And most business owners either do not have a listing at all, or have one that is incomplete and working against them.

What actually is Google Maps for business?

When you search for something local on Google, for example "coffee shop near me" or "web designer in Thessaloniki", Google shows a map with business listings before any website results. This section is called the Local Pack, and it gets more clicks than almost anything else on the page.

To appear there, you need a Google Business Profile. It is free to create, takes about 20 minutes to set up, and can bring in customers every single day without you spending a cent on advertising.

What kind of businesses need it?

The short answer is: almost all of them. But it is especially important if you are a:

  • Restaurant, café or bar
  • Hotel, guesthouse or holiday rental
  • Salon, clinic or any service you visit in person
  • Local shop or boutique
  • Tradesperson or contractor who serves a specific area

Even if most of your business comes from referrals or social media, people will still Google you before they call. If they cannot find you, they will call someone else.

What does a good Google Maps listing do for you?

A complete Google Business Profile shows your address, opening hours, phone number, photos, reviews and a link to your website. When someone finds you there, they already have everything they need to become a customer. No scrolling, no searching, no friction.

Reviews matter more than most people realise. A business with 50 reviews and a 4.7 star rating will almost always get more calls than one with no reviews at all, even if the second business is better. Google uses reviews as a signal of trust, and so do customers.

What happens if you have no listing?

Your competitors get your customers. It really is that simple. If someone searches for what you offer and you are not there, they will click on whoever is. A missing or incomplete Google Maps listing is one of the most common and most costly mistakes we see small businesses make.

How do you get listed?

Go to business.google.com, create an account and fill in your details. Google will send you a verification code by post or phone. Once verified, your listing goes live and you can start appearing in local searches.

Make sure you add real photos, write a proper description of what you do, and set your opening hours correctly. These details directly affect how high you appear in results.
