Instagram is not a website. Here is why you need both.

12 May 2026 4 min read Novo Web Build

We hear it all the time. "We have Instagram, we do not really need a website." It is understandable. Instagram is free, everyone is on it, and it looks great. But using it as your main business presence is one of the most common mistakes small businesses make online.

Here is the thing. Instagram and a website do completely different jobs. You need both, and understanding why will change how you think about your online presence.

Instagram gets you seen. Google gets you found.

When someone already knows your business exists, they might find you on Instagram. But when someone searches "hotel in Santorini" or "web designer near me" or "best restaurant in Thessaloniki", they are going to Google. And Google does not show Instagram profiles in those results. It shows websites.

If you do not have a website, you are invisible to everyone searching for what you offer. That is a huge number of potential customers you will never reach.

You do not own your Instagram

This is the part most people do not think about until it is too late. Your Instagram account belongs to Meta, not to you. Your account can be hacked, reported, suspended or simply caught in an algorithm change that kills your reach overnight. It has happened to thousands of businesses.

"My account was hacked and I lost 12,000 followers overnight. Three years of content, gone. I had no website, no email list, nothing." This is something we hear from new clients more often than you would think.

Your website, on the other hand, belongs to you completely. The domain is yours. The content is yours. No algorithm decides whether your customers can see it.

Instagram cannot do what a website does

There are things a website does that Instagram simply cannot replicate:

  • Show up in Google search results
  • Give customers your full menu, pricing or service list in an organised way
  • Take bookings or enquiries directly
  • Build credibility and trust with people who do not know you yet
  • Track exactly where your customers come from and what they do

A customer who finds you on Instagram has already decided they are interested. A customer who finds you on Google is actively looking to buy. That is a very different kind of visitor.

They work better together

The best approach is to use Instagram to build awareness and a community, and use your website to convert that interest into bookings, enquiries or sales. Your Instagram bio links to your website. Your website builds trust. Your website closes the deal.

Think of Instagram as your shop window and your website as the shop itself. You need both. A window with no shop behind it does not sell anything.

What does it actually cost?

A professionally built website for a small business starts from around €500. The final price depends on how many pages you need, what tools or features are involved, and how polished you want the result to be. A simple 3-page site is very different from a full multi-service platform with booking systems and animations. Either way, a good website typically pays for itself within the first few months. It is one of the best investments a small business can make.
