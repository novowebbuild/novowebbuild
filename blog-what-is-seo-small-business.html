What is SEO and why
it matters for your business

📅 28 January 2025 · ⏱ 4 min read · ✍️ Novo Web Build

SEO. Three letters that small business owners hear constantly and nobody ever explains properly. Agencies use it to sound smart and justify €800/month retainers. Instagram "gurus" use it as a buzzword. Most people nod along pretending they know what it means.

Let me strip it down. SEO = Search Engine Optimisation. It's the stuff you do so that when someone googles what you sell, your website shows up. That's it. That's the whole game.

Why it actually matters

Think about how you find businesses. You type something into Google. You click one of the top 3 results. You almost never scroll to the second page, be honest, when was the last time you did?

Now flip it. Your customers are doing the exact same thing. Right now, today, someone is googling what you sell. If you're not on page one, you don't exist to them. They're booking your competitor instead. SEO is how you stop losing those customers.

How Google actually decides who to show

Google's job is to give people the best answer for what they searched. If someone types "best tattoo artist Berlin", Google wants to show the tattoo artist most likely to make that person happy. It doesn't care about your logo or your ego. It cares about three things.

1. Relevance

Does your site actually talk about what they searched for? If someone searches "vegan bakery Athens" and your site just says "we make cakes" without ever using the word vegan, Google has no way to know you're the right answer. You have to actually say the words people are searching for.

2. Trust

Is your site legit? Google looks at how long you've been online, whether other real websites link to you, whether people stay on your site once they land, whether your site loads fast and works on phones. New sites start with zero trust. That's why SEO takes months to work, you're earning credibility.

3. Experience

«A site that loads in 6 seconds on mobile is already losing to one that loads in 1.»

Google tracks everything. Slow site? You drop in rankings. Confusing layout? Drop. Broken on mobile? Massive drop. Good design isn't just pretty, it's literally an SEO factor now.

The 3 things to get right before paying anyone

Before you hire an SEO "specialist" for €500/month, make sure your site has these. If it doesn't, no amount of SEO work will help.

  • Fast loading, under 2 seconds on mobile. Test yours at pagespeed.web.dev.
  • Mobile-friendly, 70% of searches happen on phones. If your site looks broken on a phone, you're done.
  • Clear content, every page should have a clear purpose, use the actual words your customers would search, and answer their question without them having to dig.

The honest truth about SEO timelines

Anyone promising you "page one in 30 days" is lying. Real SEO takes 3 to 6 months minimum to start showing results, and 12+ months to really pay off. But once it pays off, it keeps paying, unlike ads, which stop the second you stop paying.
